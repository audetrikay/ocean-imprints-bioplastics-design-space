# Discussion

The results show that bioplastics do not disappear in marine-like conditions.

Observed effects:
- rigidification  
- deformation  
- crystallization  
- persistence  

The behavior depends on:
- formulation (glycerine)  
- environment (salinity)  
- exposure (time, method)  

The project originates from workshops with children, where bioplastics were used as a replacement for conventional plastics.
During these workshops, a key question emerged:

> “If it’s made from algae, can we throw it in the sea?”

BUT natural origin ≠ safe disposal  

---

## Implication
Bioplastics are relevant only if their use and end-of-life are considered together because sustainability is not a property of the material itself but of the system in which it exist 

---

## Limits of the Study

- Simplified seawater model  
- No biological degradation considered  
- Short experimental timeframe  
- Qualitative observations  

---

## Design Implications

The results suggest that bioplastics should not be used indiscriminately, their relevance depends on the context and their end-of-life must be considered during design
