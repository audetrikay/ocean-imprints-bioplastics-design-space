# Future Work

## Research

### Objective  : better understand how alginate reacts to marine ionic environments.
 
- Isolate ion effects:
  - Ca²⁺ => crosslinking, rigidification  
  - Na⁺ => structural destabilization  
- Study long-term exposure:
  - observe evolution beyond a few days  
  - identify stable vs transient states  
- Quantify transformations:
  - flexibility (bending tests)  
  - deformation (shape change)  
  - resistance  

### Method  
- Controlled solutions with single ions  
- Time-series experiments  
- Comparative analysis between conditions  

### Outcome  
A clearer understanding of transformation mechanisms, beyond qualitative observation.

## Design Tool

### Concept : a material library of transformations documenting how bioplastics behave under different conditions.

### Content  
For each sample:
- recipe (alginate, glycerine ratio)  
- environment (freshwater, seawater)  
- exposure (time, method)  
- observed transformations (visual + mechanical)  

### Format  
- visual atlas (images + descriptions)  
- indexed by parameters  
- comparable samples  

### Purpose  
- Help designers anticipate material behavior  
- Support decision-making:
  => which material for which context  
- Shift from “material as object” => “material as evolving system”

---

## Artistic Installation

### Concept : make material transformation visible in real time.

### Setup  
- bioplastic films or shapes  
- continuous or cyclic exposure to saltwater  
- controlled environment (light, humidity, evaporation)  

### Dynamics  
- progressive deformation  
- salt crystallization  
- loss of transparency  
- rigidification over time  

### Experience  
- the audience observes slow transformation  
- contrast between expectation (biodegradable) and reality  

### Intention  
Reveal that materials do not disappear, but evolve and persist.
