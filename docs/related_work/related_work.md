# Related Work

## Bioplastics and material behavior

Research on bioplastics highlights that their properties depend on:
- polymer type (alginate, agar, starch)
- plasticizers (e.g. glycerine)
- environmental conditions

Studies show that these materials are highly sensitive to water and humidity, which affects their mechanical stability.

---

## Alginate and ionic interactions

Alginate is widely studied for its ability to interact with ions:

- Ca²⁺ ions create crosslinks → increase rigidity  
- Na⁺ ions can destabilize the structure  

These mechanisms are commonly used in biomedical and material applications.

---

## Marine environment and plastics

Research on plastics in marine environments shows that:
- degradation is slow  
- UV exposure is reduced underwater  
- environmental conditions alter materials rather than eliminating them  

However, most studies focus on conventional plastics, not bioplastics.

---

## Gap

- Bioplastics are mainly studied in compost or lab conditions  
- Few studies explore their behavior in marine environments  
- Even fewer focus on visible transformations  

---

## Contribution of this project

This project proposes:

- A **design-oriented exploration** of bioplastic behavior  
- A focus on **transformation instead of degradation**  
- A **visual material atlas** under marine-like conditions  

---

## Positioning

At the intersection of:
- material science  
- design research  
- environmental context

## Key idea

> Understanding material behavior requires observing it in real conditions, not only in ideal scenarios.
