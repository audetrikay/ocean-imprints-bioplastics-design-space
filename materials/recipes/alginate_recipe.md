# Alginate Bioplastic Recipe

## Overview

This protocol describes how to produce alginate-based bioplastic films with adjustable mechanical properties using glycerine as a plasticizer.

Two formulations are used:
- Low glycerine → rigid films  
- High glycerine → flexible films  

---

## Materials

- Sodium alginate: 2–3 g  
- Distilled water: 100 mL  
- Glycerine (plasticizer):  
  - 2 g → rigid film  
  - 6 g → flexible film  
- Calcium lactate (crosslinking agent)  
- Optional: food coloring  

---

## Equipment

- Beaker or container  
- Stirrer or blender  
- Flat casting surface (plate, mold)  
- Scale  
- Spray bottle  

---

## Step 1 — Preparation of the solution

1. Pour 100 mL of distilled water into a container  
2. Gradually add sodium alginate while stirring  
3. Mix until homogeneous (no lumps)  
4. Add glycerine and mix thoroughly  
5. Add coloring if needed  

---

## Step 2 — Casting the film

1. Pour the solution onto a flat surface  
2. Spread evenly to control thickness  
3. Remove bubbles if necessary  
4. Let dry at room temperature for 24–48 hours  

---

## Step 3 — Crosslinking (stabilization)

Alginate requires calcium ions to form a stable structure.

### Method

1. Prepare a calcium lactate solution (5–10 g/L in water)  
2. Spray the solution onto the dried film  
3. Wait a few minutes for crosslinking  

### Mechanism

Calcium ions (Ca²⁺) create ionic bridges between alginate chains → forming a gel network.

---

## Parameters and effects

| Parameter | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| Low glycerine | Rigid, brittle film |
| High glycerine | Flexible, elastic film |
| Thickness | Influences drying time and strength |
| Calcium amount | Controls rigidity |

---

## Notes

- Films are hydrophilic → sensitive to water  
- Mechanical properties depend strongly on formulation  
- Small variations in glycerine significantly change behavior  

---

## Relevance for the project

This recipe allows the production of controlled samples to study how initial material properties influence transformations in marine environments.
