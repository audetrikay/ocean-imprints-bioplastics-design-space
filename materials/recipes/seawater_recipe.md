# Artificial Seawater Recipe

## Overview

This artificial seawater solution is used to simulate marine conditions in a controlled environment.

It is based on the ASTM D1141-98 standard, but adapted to amplify interactions with alginate-based bioplastics.

---

## Reference (ASTM Standard)

Typical composition for 1 L:

- NaCl: 24.5 g  
- MgSO₄·7H₂O: 3.87 g  
- CaCl₂: 2.16 g  
- NaHCO₃: ~0.2 g  
- KCl: ~0.7 g  
- Na₂HPO₄: ~0.1 g  
- Distilled water: up to 1 L  

---

## Modified Recipe (used in this project)

To enhance visible transformations:

- Sodium chloride (NaCl): 40 g  
- Magnesium sulfate (MgSO₄·7H₂O): 6 g  
- Calcium lactate (E327): 5 g  
- Disodium hydrogen phosphate (Na₂HPO₄): 0.5 g  
- Distilled water: up to 1 L  

---

## Preparation

1. Pour ~800 mL of distilled water into a container  
2. Add salts one by one while stirring:
   - NaCl  
   - MgSO₄  
   - Calcium lactate  
   - Na₂HPO₄  
3. Stir until fully dissolved  
4. Add water to reach 1 L  
5. Mix thoroughly  

---

## Key parameter: Calcium ions (Ca²⁺)

Calcium plays a central role in this project.

### Interaction with alginate

- Alginate forms crosslinks with Ca²⁺ ions  
- This creates a 3D network (gel structure)  
- Leads to **rigidification of the material**

---

## Why modify the ASTM recipe?

This project does not aim to perfectly replicate ocean conditions.

Instead, it aims to:

- Accelerate chemical interactions  
- Make transformations visible in a short time  
- Create a **functional experimental model**

---

## Expected effects on bioplastics

- Ionic exchange  
- Structural reorganization  
- Increased rigidity  
- Salt crystallization (especially during evaporation)  

---

## Validation

- pH ≈ 8 (close to natural seawater)  
- Presence of main ions found in ocean water  
- Increased concentration for experimental visibility  

---

## Notes

- This is a simplified model of seawater  
- Results are qualitative (not exact ocean simulation)  
- Suitable for design and material exploration purposes  

---

## Relevance for the project

This solution allows controlled observation of how marine-like ionic conditions affect bioplastic materials, revealing transformation mechanisms rather than degradation.
