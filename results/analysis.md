# Analysis

## Overview

This analysis focuses on how alginate bioplastics transform under different conditions:
- glycerine concentration  
- exposure method  
- exposure intensity  

---

## Effect of Environment

### Seawater

- induces structural changes  
- increases rigidity over time  
- modifies material cohesion  

### Freshwater (control)

- swelling observed  
- no crystallization  
- structure remains stable  

---

## Effect of Method

### Immersion

- transformation occurs throughout the material  
- progressive rigidification  
- deformation increases with time  

### Vaporization

- surface-level transformation  
- salt crystallization  
- rough texture and opacity  

---

## Effect of Exposure

### Immersion (time)

- 6h → slight changes  
- 2d → visible deformation and rigidity  
- 6d → strong rigidification, brittle behavior  

### Vaporization (sprays)

- 1 spray → limited effect  
- 3 sprays → crystallization and surface modification  

---

## Effect of Glycerine

### Low glycerine (2g)

- initially rigid  
- becomes brittle faster  
- more sensitive to deformation  

### High glycerine (6g)

- initially flexible  
- maintains flexibility longer  
- gradual rigidification  

---

## Cross-effects

- seawater + immersion + time → structural transformation  
- seawater + vaporization → crystallization  
- high glycerine delays rigidification  
- low glycerine accelerates material failure  

---

## Key Insight

Bioplastics do not disappear in marine-like conditions.

They undergo:
- structural transformation  
- rigidification  
- crystallization  

---

## Conclusion

Material behavior depends on the combination of:
- formulation  
- environment  
- exposure conditions  

Transformation intensity increases with exposure.
